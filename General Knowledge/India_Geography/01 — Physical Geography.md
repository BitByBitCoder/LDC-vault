## **Plateaus** — Deccan Plateau, Chota Nagpur, Malwa

| **Plateau**                      | **Location**                                      | **Key Feature**                                                                                                   | **Formed by**                                                                  | **Exam Trap**                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| -------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Deccan Plateau**               | South of Narmada, between Western & Eastern Ghats | Largest plateau; black/regur soil; major cotton belt                                                              | Basaltic lava flows via fissure eruptions (Deccan Traps, ~66 mya)              | **Trap 1:** Mistaken as covering _all_ Peninsular India (it is only one sub-division).<br><br>  <br><br>**Trap 2:** Assuming rivers flow west; except Narmada & Tapi, major rivers flow **east**.          |
| **Chotanagpur Plateau**          | Jharkhand, Odisha, Chhattisgarh, W. Bengal        | "Mineral heartland" (Ruhr of India) — rich in coal, iron ore, mica, bauxite                                       | Ancient Precambrian gneiss/schist rock + Gondwana coal beds                    | **Trap 1:** Confused with the Deccan Trap (it is much older and non-volcanic).<br><br>  <br><br>**Trap 2:** Contains Rajmahal hills on its northeast, which _are_ basaltic.                                |
| **Malwa Plateau**                | Madhya Pradesh, SE Rajasthan                      | Rolling plateau between Aravallis & Vindhyas; famous for Chambal badland/ravine topography                        | Volcanic lava flows (northern spread of Deccan Traps) covered with black soil  | **Trap 1:** Rivers (Chambal, Betwa, Ken) flow **North/Northeast** to Yamuna, following the slope away from Vindhyas.<br><br>  <br><br>**Trap 2:** Alluvium is minimal; main soil is **black cotton soil**. |
| **Marwar / Mewar Plateau**       | Eastern Rajasthan (East of Aravallis)             | Rolling plain carved out by Banas River & tributaries                                                             | Vindhyan sedimentary rocks (sandstone, shale, limestone)                       | **Trap 1:** Confused with the **Marwar Plain**, which lies _west_ of the Aravallis (Thar Desert).<br><br>  <br><br>**Trap 2:** Streams flow into Chambal, not directly into the sea.                       |
| **Meghalaya (Shillong) Plateau** | Meghalaya                                         | Khasi, Garo, Jaintia Hills; site of Mawsynram / Cherrapunji                                                       | Extension of Peninsular shield, separated by **Garo-Rajmahal Gap (Malda Gap)** | **Trap 1:** Frequently misgrouped with the Himalayas (it is purely Peninsular block).<br><br>  <br><br>**Trap 2:** Believing the Malda Gap is structural; it was filled by Ganga-Brahmaputra silt.         |
| **Karnataka (Mysore) Plateau**   | Karnataka & parts of Kerala/TN                    | Divided into **Malnad** (hilly forest) and **Maidan** (rolling plain); home to Bababudan Hills (Iron ore, Coffee) | Archaean gneisses & granites (northern part has some basaltic lava)            | **Trap 1:** Divided into two distinct physiographic zones (Malnad vs Maidan).<br><br>  <br><br>**Trap 2:** Origin of major east-flowing rivers (Kaveri, Tungabhadra)                                       |

|Plain|Location|Formed by|Key sub-regions/rivers|Key facts|Exam trap|
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

|**Terai belt**|South of Bhabar|Re-emerged underground streams|—|Marshy, wet, forested; malaria-prone historically|—|
|**Delta region**|Ganga-Brahmaputra mouth, W. Bengal/Bangladesh|Deltaic deposition|Sundarbans|World's largest delta; mangrove forests|Sometimes tested as part of coastal plain — it's actually Gangetic plain's continuation|
|**Western Coastal Plain**|From Gujarat to Kerala (west coast)|Narrow, submergent coastline|Konkan coast (Maharashtra-Goa), Kannad coast (Karnataka), Malabar coast (Kerala)|Narrower than east coast; few large deltas; Western Ghats close to coast|Narrow because it's a **submerged** coast (land sank)|
|**Eastern Coastal Plain**|From West Bengal to Tamil Nadu (east coast)|Broader, emergent coastline|Northern Circars (Odisha-AP), Coromandel Coast (TN-AP)|Wider than west coast; major deltas — Mahanadi, Godavari, Krishna, Kaveri|Wider because it's an **emergent** coast (land rose) — opposite mechanism from west coast|

|Feature|Andaman & Nicobar|Lakshadweep|
|---|---|---|
|Ocean|Bay of Bengal|Arabian Sea|

**Memory anchor:** Bay of Bengal islands = mountains poking through water (volcanic/tectonic); Arabian Sea islands = coral built up from the seafloor. Origin-type questions ("which island group is of coral origin") are the single most repeated pattern here.

## Straits & Water Bodies (India **Straits, Channels & Water Passages** )

## Exam Traps ⚠️
> Highest in India = **Kanchenjunga** (Sikkim) — NOT Everest (Nepal)
> Highest in Peninsular India = **Anaimudi** (Kerala) — NOT Nilgiri
> Ghats meet = **Nilgiri** — NOT Annamalai or Palani
> Nathu La = **Sikkim** — NOT Arunachal or Himachal
> Umling La = **highest motorable** — Khardung La is NOT highest anymore
> India's only active volcano = **Barren Island, Andaman** — NOT Lakshadweep
