## Crops — Kharif vs Rabi

| Kharif (Summer/Monsoon) — sown June, harvested Oct | Rabi (Winter) — sown Oct, harvested March |
|---------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| Rice, Cotton, Maize, Jowar, Bajra | Wheat, Gram, Pea, Mustard, Barley |
| Sugarcane, Groundnut, Soybean | Linseed, Oilseeds |
| **Sown with onset of monsoon (June)** | **Sown in winter (October–November)** |

> 💡 **Exam trap:** Cotton = Kharif. Wheat = Rabi. Gram = Rabi. Don't mix.

## Types of Soil in India

| Soil | States | Key Crop |
|------|--------|---------|
| **Alluvial soil** | UP, Bihar, Punjab, Haryana, Bengal | Wheat, Rice, Sugarcane |
| **Black/Regur soil** | Maharashtra, MP, Gujarat | **Cotton** |
| **Red soil** | Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, AP | Groundnut, Cotton |
| **Laterite soil** | Kerala, Karnataka, Odisha | Tea, Coffee |
| **Desert/Arid soil** | Rajasthan | — |
| **Mountain soil** | Himalayas | Tea, Coffee, Fruits |

> 💡 Red soil = **Iron Oxides** (appeared directly in papers)
> 💡 Black soil = best for **Cotton** — also called Regur soil

## Exam Traps ⚠️
> Cotton = **Kharif** — NOT Rabi
> Wheat = **Rabi** — NOT Kharif
> Golden Revolution = **Horticulture and Honey** — NOT gold mining
> Blue Revolution = **Fish** — NOT water
> Red soil colour = **Iron Oxides** — NOT Sulphur or Phosphorus
> Black soil best for = **Cotton** — found in Maharashtra (Deccan)
> Tundra = **Cold desert** — NOT tropical or deciduous
