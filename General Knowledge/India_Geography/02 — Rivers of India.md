## Exam Traps ⚠️
> Longest river India = **Ganga** — Brahmaputra is longest by volume/discharge
> Narmada = **Arabian Sea** — NOT Bay of Bengal
> Largest brackish water = **Chilika (Odisha)** — NOT Dal or Wular
> Highest salinity = **Sambhar (Rajasthan)** — NOT Chilika
> Sutlej = **Trans-Himalayan** — originates in Tibet
> Wular = largest **freshwater** lake India — Chilika = largest **brackish water**


## River system
In Indian geography exams, the **Sutlej River** is classified under three distinct types based on its origin, behavior, and river system:

### 1. Antecedent River (Trans-Himalayan River)

This is the most critical classification tested in exams. An **antecedent river** is a river that existed _before_ the mountain ranges (Himalayas) were formed.

- As the Himalayas slowly rose over millions of years, the Sutlej River didn't change its course. Instead, it cut incredibly deep valleys and steep gorges right through the rising mountains to maintain its original path.
    
- _Mizo:_ Tlang (Himalayas) a lo pian hma daiha lo luang tawh a ni a. Tlang a lo to chhoh pawhin a kawng a thlak ve chuang lo va, tlang kha a lo hriat khuar thuk tlat a ni.
    

### 2. Perennial River

Because its source is in the high-altitude glaciers of Tibet, it is a **perennial river**—meaning it flows with a heavy volume of water throughout the entire year and never dries up, unlike rain-fed peninsular rivers.

### 3. Major Tributary (Indus River System)

It is the longest and the easternmost of the **five major tributaries of the Indus River** (Panjnad), joining the Indus in Pakistan.
