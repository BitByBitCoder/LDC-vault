## Step-by-Step Method

```
Step 1: Draw the family tree on paper immediately
Step 2: Start from a known person (usually "I" or the subject)
Step 3: Add each relationship as you read
Step 4: Find the path from person A to person B
Step 5: Name that relationship
```

---

## Classic Questions — Worked Solutions

### Q1: "She is the daughter of my grandfather's only son"
```
My grandfather's only son = my Father
Father's daughter = my Sister
Answer: Sister
```

### Q2: "Brothers and sisters I have none, but the father of the person in the picture is my father's son"
```
No brothers or sisters → I am an only child
My father's son = Me (since no brothers)
Father of person = Me
So the person in picture = My Son
Answer: Her son
```

### Q3: "Woman introduces man as son of brother of her mother"
```
Her mother's brother = her Maternal Uncle
Maternal Uncle's son = her Cousin
Answer: Cousin
```

### Q4: O's mother is sister of P and daughter of M. S is daughter of P and sister of T. How is M related to T?
```
Draw tree:
M → O's mother (M is parent of O's mother)
O's mother is sister of P → P and O's mother are siblings under M
P → S and T (P is parent of S and T)
M is grandparent of S and T
Answer: Maternal grandfather or grandmother
```

## Coded Blood Relations
Some questions use symbols:
- A + B means A is the mother of B
- A − B means A is the brother of B
- A × B means A is the father of B
- A ÷ B means A is the sister of B

**Method:** Convert symbol to relationship words, then draw tree

---

## Exam Traps ⚠️
> "Father's son" when no siblings stated = **yourself**
> "Mother's brother" = **Maternal Uncle** (not just Uncle — type matters sometimes)
> Cousin = child of any Uncle or Aunt
> "Grandfather's only son" = your Father (if you have no uncles on that side)
> Always draw the tree — never solve blood relation in your head
> "Sister of T" and "daughter of P" means T is also child of P — same generation as T
