**Mizo Culture, Tradition & Folklore**

- Cheraw dance — origin (comfort soul of deceased mother)
- Chapchar Kut — spring festival, bamboo clearing
- Pawl Kut — thanksgiving after harvest
- Mim Kut — after corn harvest
- Thalfavang Kut — end of harvest season
- Mautam — bamboo flowering famine (1959, 2006–2008)
- Traditional dress — Puan, Kawrchhi, Puanchei
- Puithiam — priest who performs sacrificial rites
- Bengbung — part of female dress
- Traditional traps, baskets, instruments
- Lasi, Khuavang — supernatural beings in Mizo mythology
- Famous folktales — Sichangneii, Kungawrhi, Lalruanga, Phawthira

**Mizoram Geography & Facts**

- 11 districts and their headquarters
- Borders: Assam, Manipur, Tripura (states) + Myanmar, Bangladesh (countries)
- Highest peak: Phawngpui / Blue Mountain (Lunglei district)
- Sur Tlang — third highest peak (Serchhip)
- Rivers: Tlawng, Tuichang, Tuirial
- Tropic of Cancer passes through Mizoram
- Population rank among small states (Census 2011)

**Mizo Literature & Awards**

- Mizo Award 2025 — B. Lalthangliana

**Mizoram Legislature & Governance**

#### Constitutional Amendments (appeared in papers)

|Amendment|What it did|
|---|---|
|42nd (1976)|Added Fundamental Duties, Secular + Socialist to Preamble|
|44th (1978)|Restored original provisions changed by 42nd|

|74th (1992)|Urban local bodies — Municipalities|

#### Key Facts

- President resigns to → **Vice President**
- PM resigns to → **President**
- Number of ministers including PM shall not exceed → **15% of Lok Sabha strength**
- Part IV of Constitution → **Directive Principles of State Policy**
- Part III → **Fundamental Rights**
- Part IVA → **Fundamental Duties**
- Magna Carta of Indian people → **Government of India Act, 1858**
- Father of civil services in India → **Lord Cornwallis**
- Panchayati Raj opposed by → **B.R. Ambedkar**
- 6th Schedule → Administration of tribal areas in Assam, Meghalaya, Tripura, Mizoram
