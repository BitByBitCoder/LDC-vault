It can definitely feel like you're voting a lot, especially since all these administrative lines overlap! It helps to think of it like zooming in on a digital map—going from the whole state, down to your city, and finally right down to your exact neighborhood (_Veng_).

Here is exactly how the political hierarchy and the geographical areas break down when you cast your vote in Aizawl:

## 1. The State Level: MLA Elections

- **The Area:** **Assembly Constituency**
    
- **How it works:** The entire state of Mizoram is divided into 40 big geographic pieces called Assembly Constituencies. Aizawl district is split into multiple constituencies (such as _Aizawl East-I, Aizawl West-I, Aizawl North-I_, etc.). Multiple neighborhoods are lumped together to make just one constituency.
    
- **Who you vote for:** You vote for one **MLA** (Member of Legislative Assembly) to represent your entire section of the city in the state government.
    
- **The Big Picture:** When the Zoram People's Movement (ZPM) won the majority of these 40 seats, they formed the state government to run Mizoram.
    

## 2. The City Level: AMC Elections

- **The Area:** **Municipal Ward**
    
- **How it works:** Aizawl city has its own urban civic government called the **Aizawl Municipal Corporation (AMC)**. For this, the city map is divided into **19 Wards**. A Ward is smaller than an MLA constituency but bigger than a single neighborhood—it usually combines 2 to 5 neighboring _Vengs_.
    
- **Who you vote for:** You vote for a single **Ward Councillor (Corporator)** to represent your specific Ward.
    
- **The Big Picture:** They look after city-wide issues like municipal roads, streetlights, town planning, and major waste management. In the April 2026 AMC elections, ZPM won a landslide victory, securing 17 out of the 19 ward seats.
    

## 3. The Grassroots Level: Local Council Elections

- **The Area:** **Your Specific Veng (Locality)**
    
- **How it works:** This is the smallest, most immediate boundary. Every individual neighborhood inside the Aizawl municipality (like _Chanmari, Mission Veng, Bawngkawn, or Ramhlun_) is its own distinct unit.
    
- **Who you vote for:** Instead of just voting for one person, you vote for a small team of **5 to 7 Local Council Members** who live right in your neighborhood.
    
- **The Big Picture:** They handle day-to-day things right outside your doorstep—local sanitation, neighborhood cleaning drives, distribution of specific local schemes, and coordinating community matters with local NGOs like the YMA (Young Mizo Association).
    

_(Note: If you step outside Aizawl city limits into rural areas of Mizoram, this exact grassroots tier is called a **Village Council** instead of a Local Council)._

### Summary of Aizawl's Voting Hierarchy

|**Governance Level**|**The Boundary / Area You Vote In**|**Who You Are Electing**|**What They Do**|
|---|---|---|---|
|**State Government**|**Assembly Constituency** (Large area; groups many Vengs together)|**MLA** (Member of Legislative Assembly)|Pass state laws, manage the state budget, and oversee state-wide infrastructure.|
|**City Government (AMC)**|**Municipal Ward** (Medium area; groups 2 to 5 neighboring Vengs)|**Ward Councillor / Corporator**|Look after city-level development, trash collection systems, and urban markets.|
|**Grassroots Level**|**Your Veng / Locality** (Smallest area; just your neighborhood)|**Local Council Members** (A team of 5 to 7 people)|Handle immediate local welfare, neighborhood disputes, and local sanitation.|

The financial breakdown for his role details both components alongside how the annual budget works:

### 1. His Take-Home Salary

Because Pu C. Lalsawivunga is a **Cabinet Minister** alongside his role as an MLA, he falls into a higher state government pay tier than a regular legislator.

- **Monthly Salary & Allowances:** He receives a total of **₹1.68 lakh per month**.
    
- **Perks:** As a minister, this package includes standard government-provided benefits such as an official furnished bungalow, a state vehicle with an assigned driver, official fuel allowances, and full medical coverage.
    

_(For comparison, a regular Mizoram MLA who does not hold a ministerial portfolio receives around ₹1.50 lakh per month)._

### 2. His Official Constituency Development Funds

This is public money meant strictly for local development works. He cannot spend a single rupee of this on personal expenses.

- **The Fund Name:** MLA Local Area Development Scheme (MLALADS).
    
- **The Allocation:** He is allotted exactly **₹2 crore (₹2,00,00,000) per year** to spend across the entire Aizawl South-I constituency.
    

### 3. The One-Year Budget Cycle

**Yes, the funds operate strictly on a one-year financial budget cycle** (running from April 1st to March 31st of the following year).

The workflow of his yearly budget operates through a specific process:

- **The Allocation:** Every year, right after the Mizoram Legislative Assembly passes the state's main budget, this ₹2 crore allocation is formally approved.
    
- **The Guardrails:** The money is never handed to the MLA directly. Instead, it is placed in a dedicated account managed by the **Deputy Commissioner (DC) of Aizawl District**.
    
- **The Execution:** When a neighborhood project is identified (e.g., repairing retaining walls or footpaths in Republic Veng), Pu C. Lalsawivunga submits a formal recommendation to the DC office. The DC checks the rules, ensures the project costs at least ₹1 lakh, and pays the executing agency directly.
    

The State Planning Department monitors these annual funds closely; if an MLA fails to show progress reports on how the first half of the year's budget was spent, the next installment can be held back.
