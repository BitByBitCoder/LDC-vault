#### 1. Famous Tragic Love Stories (Thawnthu Inhmangaih)

These are often asked about in literature and culture sections:

- **Zawlpala and Tualvungi** (features the antagonist Phuntiha).
    
- **Chawngvungi and Sawngkhara.**
    
- **Lianchhiari and Chawngfianga.**
    
- **Tlingi and Ngama.**
    

#### 2. Legendary Heroes & Men

These stories often highlight strength, wit, or specific cultural motifs:

- **Chhura:** The most famous Mizo folk hero. Known for being funny, witty, and sometimes foolish (the "Chhura's Magic Horn" or "Chhura and the Chengkek Fruit" stories are very popular).
    
- **Liandova and Tuaisiala:** A story about two brothers and their struggles.
    
- **Chemtatrawta:** Known for his unique personality and tales.
    
- **Hrangchala and Phawthira:** Often associated with bravery.
    

#### 3. Heroines & Mythical Maidens

- **Kungawrhi:** A famous tale of a girl who is abducted and rescued.
    
- **Sichangneii:** A classic tale of a maiden's journey.
    
- **Mauruangi:** A popular story often studied for its moral and cultural themes.
    
- **Chawngchilhi:** A well-known figure in Mizo folklore.
    

#### 4. Spirits & Nature (Mythology)

These stories explain the natural world and the spirit realm:

- **Lasi:** The guardian spirits of wild animals.
    
- **Khuavang:** Benevolent forest spirits.
    
- **Pheichham:** The supernatural one-legged being.
