## 🎭 Sub-Topic 4 — Culture & Tradition

### Festivals
Here is the corrected table for the festivals and their occasions:

|**Festival**|**Occasion / Significance**|
|---|---|
|**Chapchar Kut**|Spring festival — celebrated after _jhum_ clearing (bamboo cutting) is complete.|
|**Pawl Kut**|Thanksgiving festival — celebrated after all main crops have been safely harvested.|
|**Mim Kut**|Maize/corn harvest festival — dedicated to honoring deceased loved ones with the first fruits.|
|**Thalfavang Kut**|Pre-harvest festival — celebrated after the completion of weeding, serving as a rest period before heavy harvesting begins.|

## 📚 Sub-Topic 6 — Mizo Literature & Awards

The information you provided contains several inaccuracies. Below is the corrected version for your notes:

| **Question**                                  | **Answer**                                                                                                             |
| --------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **First Mizo novel**                          | _Hawilopari_ (1936) by L. Biakliana                                                                                    |
| **Mizo Chanchin — author**                    | Rev. Liangkhaia                                                                                                        |
| **MAL Book of the Year 2021**                 | _Hnam Ropuite U, tho r'u le!_ by Lalhruaitluanga Chawngte                                                              |
| **Mizo Award 2025**                           | _There is no single "Mizo Award" by that name; you may be thinking of individual state honors or professional awards._ |
| **First awardee of Mizo Award**               | _See note below._                                                                                                      |
| **Only Padma Bhushan from Mizoram**           | _There are multiple recipients of the Padma Bhushan from Mizoram._                                                     |
| **Esther Hnamte (PM-Rashtriya Bal Puraskar)** | Awarded December 2025 in the Art & Culture category                                                                    |
| **S.T. Rualyapa (field)**                     | Politics                                                                                                               |
|                                               |                                                                                                                        |

### Key Clarifications:

- **Padma Bhushan:** There is no "only" Padma Bhushan recipient from Mizoram; several notable individuals from the state have been honored with this award over the years.
    
- **"Mizo Award":** There is no singular national or state honor officially titled the "Mizo Award." You may be referring to specific literary or academic awards like the _Mizo Academy of Letters Book of the Year_, or perhaps a different honor.
    
- **First Awardee:** Because there is no "Mizo Award," there is no first awardee by that name. If you meant the first Padma Shri recipient from Mizoram (often a point of confusion), it was **Nuchhungi Renthlei** (though she was the first Mizo _woman_ to receive it, she was the third overall from the state).
    
- **S.T. Rualyapa:** You are correct; he was a political figure. Questions regarding his association with the field of "Politics" appear in recent departmental exam papers in Mizoram.
