
Here are the crucial "Exam Traps" and nuances to remember from your data, along with their explanations in Mizo:

### 1. President vs. Vice President (Inthlan chiah hian an danglam)

- **The Trap:** Examiners love to confuse who votes for whom.
    
- **Mizo Nuance:**
    
    - **President** inthlannaah (Electoral College) hian Parliament leh State Legislative Assembly-a **Elected Members** chauhvin vote an thlak thei. _Nominated members_ (MP leh MLA ruat chawpte) an tel ve thei lo.
        
    - **Vice President** inthlannaah erawh chuan Parliament (Lok Sabha & Rajya Sabha) member zawng zawng (_Elected leh Nominated_) an tel vek thung a, mahse **State MLAs te an tel ve thung lo**.
        

### 2. Impeachment vs. Removal (Ban dan kawng)

- **The Trap:** The term "Impeachment" is constitutionally reserved _only_ for the President (Article 61).
    
- **Mizo Nuance:**
    
    - **President** ban tur hian "Impeachment" thupui hnuaiah Parliament House pahnihah **2/3 of Total Membership** (House-a thutna awm zawng zawng hmun thuma thena hmun hnih) thlawpna a ngai a, a khirh hle.
        
    - **Vice President, Speaker, leh Deputy Speaker** erawh "Effective Majority" hmangin ban an ni thin.
        

### 3. What is "Effective Majority"? (Hriat thiam tur pawimawh)

- **Mizo Nuance:** "Effective Majority" awmzia chu — House-a thutna ruak (vacant seats chhan hrang hrang vanga mi boral/bâng) zawng zawng paih hnu lama **member la awm hnaivai zawng zawng zat ve aia tam** (Majority of all the 'then' members) tihna a ni.
    

### 4. Lok Sabha Speaker's Term (Thutna chelh chhung)

- **The Trap:** When Lok Sabha dissolves, does the Speaker step down immediately? No.
    
- **Mizo Nuance:** Lok Sabha a thiah (dissolve) pawhin Speaker hi a bang ve nghal mai lo. Lok Sabha thar lo thut khawm hma chiah thleng khan a hna a thawk chhunzawm char char thin.
