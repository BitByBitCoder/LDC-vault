## Questions From Your Papers

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Citizenship — Part of Constitution | **Part II (Articles 5–11)** |
| Single citizenship borrowed from | **UK (Britain)** |
| India follows | **Single citizenship** (NOT dual) |
| Citizenship Act | **Citizenship Act 1955** |
| OCI — Overseas Citizen of India | NOT full citizenship — limited rights |
| CAA — Citizenship Amendment Act | **2019** |
| CAA gives citizenship to | Persecuted minorities from Pakistan, Bangladesh, Afghanistan (NOT Muslims) |

## Ways to Acquire Citizenship (Citizenship Act 1955)

| Method | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **By Birth** | Born in India |
| **By Descent** | Parents are Indian citizens |
| **By Registration** | Apply and register |
| **By Naturalisation** | Lived in India for 11 years |
| **By Incorporation of Territory** | If new territory joins India |

## Loss of Citizenship

| Method | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Renunciation** | Voluntarily gives up |
| **Termination** | Acquires citizenship of another country |
| **Deprivation** | Government cancels (fraud etc.) |

## Exam Traps ⚠️
> India = **Single citizenship** (like UK) — NOT dual (like USA)
> Naturalisation requires **11 years** residency
> OCI = **NOT full citizenship** — cannot vote or hold constitutional posts
> CAA 2019 gives citizenship to **non-Muslim minorities** from 3 countries only
