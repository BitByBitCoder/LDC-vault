## Questions From Your Papers

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Writs issued by SC under | **Article 32** |
| Writs issued by HC under | **Article 226** |
| Writ for illegal detention | **Habeas Corpus** |
| Writ for public/statutory duty | **Mandamus** |
| Writ to quash lower court order | **Certiorari** |
| Writ to stop lower court proceedings | **Prohibition** |
| Writ to question authority/right to hold office | **Quo Warranto** |

## All 5 Writs — Complete Table

| Writ | Meaning (Latin) | Used For |
|------|-----------------|---------|
| **Habeas Corpus** | "You may have the body" | Release of illegally detained person |
| **Mandamus** | "We command" | Compel public authority to perform duty |
| **Certiorari** | "To be certified" | Quash order of lower court/tribunal |
| **Prohibition** | "To forbid" | Stop lower court from exceeding jurisdiction |
| **Quo Warranto** | "By what authority" | Question someone's right to hold public office |

## Key Differences

| Habeas Corpus | Mandamus |
|---------------|---------|
| For individuals (illegal detention) | For public bodies (duty not performed) |
| Can be issued against private persons too | Only against public authorities |

## Who Can Issue Writs

| Court | Article | Scope |
|-------|---------|-------|
| Supreme Court | **Article 32** | Enforcement of Fundamental Rights only |
| High Court | **Article 226** | Both FR and other legal rights — **wider scope** |

## Exam Traps ⚠️
> Habeas Corpus = **illegal detention** — NOT for duty
> Mandamus = **public duty** — NOT for detention
> HC writ power (**Article 226**) is **wider** than SC writ power (**Article 32**)
> Quo Warranto = about right to hold **public office** — very specific
> Prohibition = issued BEFORE decision; Certiorari = issued AFTER decision
