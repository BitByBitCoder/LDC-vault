## Attorney General vs Advocate General

| Position | Scope |
|----------|-------|
| **Attorney General of India** | Article 76 — appears in **any court in India** |
| **Solicitor General** | 2nd law officer of Government of India |
| **Advocate General of State** | Article 165 — appears in state courts only |

## CAG — Comptroller & Auditor General

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Article | 148 |
| Appointed by | President |
| Tenure | **6 years or 65 years** (whichever earlier) |
| Removal | Like SC judge (impeachment) |
| Reports submitted to | **President** (for Union) / **Governor** (for States) |
| Called | Watchdog of Public Finance |

## Exam Traps ⚠️
> NITI Aayog head = **PM** — NOT President
> Planning Commission replaced by NITI Aayog in **2015** — NOT 2014
> Attorney General = **any court** — not just SC
> CAG reports go to **President** — NOT Parliament directly (President lays them before Parliament)
> Finance Commission constituted every **5 years** — same as general elections
