Given the scope (18 topics), here's a high-yield table for each — sized to match priority level (High = more detail, Low = compact):

1. Constitution Basics 🔴

|Fact|Detail|
|---|---|
|Adopted|26 November 1949|
|Enforced|26 January 1950 (chosen to honor 1930 Purna Swaraj declaration)|
|Drafting Committee Chairman|Dr. B.R. Ambedkar|
|Constituent Assembly President|Dr. Rajendra Prasad|
|Total time taken|2 years, 11 months, 18 days|
|Original members|389 (reduced to 299 after Partition)|
|Longest written constitution|World's longest — originally 395 Articles, 22 Parts, 8 Schedules (now 12 Schedules)|
|Preamble amended|Only once — 42nd Amendment (1976) added "Socialist, Secular, Integrity"|
|Exam trap|Preamble is NOT part of original enacting text debate — but Supreme Court (Kesavananda Bharati case) ruled it IS part of the Constitution|

2. Fundamental Rights 🔴

|Article|Right|
|---|---|
|14–18|Right to Equality|
|19–22|Right to Freedom|
|23–24|Right against Exploitation|
|25–28|Right to Freedom of Religion|
|29–30|Cultural & Educational Rights|
|32|Right to Constitutional Remedies ("heart and soul" — Ambedkar's words)|
|Exam trap|Right to Property (Art. 31) removed from Fundamental Rights by 44th Amendment (1978) — now only a legal right under Art. 300A|
|Exam trap|Only 6 Fundamental Rights now, not 7 — Right to Property downgrade is the most-tested change|

3. Parliament Structure 🔴

|Feature|Lok Sabha|Rajya Sabha|
|---|---|---|
|Nature|Lower House, directly elected|Upper House, permanent body (never dissolved)|
|Max strength|552 (currently ~543 elected)|250 (238 elected + 12 nominated)|
|Term|5 years|Members serve 6 years; 1/3 retire every 2 years|
|Presiding officer|Speaker|Vice-President (ex-officio Chairman)|
|Money Bills|Can only originate here|Cannot amend/reject, only delay 14 days|
|Exam trap|Rajya Sabha members nominated by President — 12, for expertise in art/literature/science/social service||

4. President & Vice President 🔴

|Fact|Detail|
|---|---|
|Election method|Electoral college — elected MPs + elected MLAs (proportional representation, single transferable vote)|
|Term|5 years, eligible for re-election|
|Min age|35 years (President), 35 years (VP)|
|VP's role|Ex-officio Chairman of Rajya Sabha|
|Impeachment|President — Art. 61, by Parliament, for "violation of Constitution"|
|Exam trap|Nominated MPs and MLAs of UTs (except Delhi & Puducherry) do NOT vote in presidential election|

 5. PM & Council of Ministers 🔴

|Fact|Detail|
|---|---|
|Appointment|PM appointed by President; other ministers appointed by President on PM's advice|
|Collective responsibility|Council of Ministers responsible to Lok Sabha (Art. 75)|
|Max ministers|Capped at 15% of Lok Sabha strength (91st Amendment, 2003)|
|Exam trap|PM need NOT be a sitting MP at time of appointment, but must become one (either house) within 6 months|

6. Constitutional Amendments 🔴

|Amendment|Year|Key change|
|---|---|---|
|1st|1951|Added 9th Schedule; restricted freedom of speech grounds|
|42nd|1976|"Mini-Constitution" — added Socialist/Secular/Integrity to Preamble, added Fundamental Duties|
|44th|1978|Removed Right to Property from Fundamental Rights|
|61st|1989|Reduced voting age 21→18|
|73rd/74th|1992|Panchayati Raj & Municipalities given constitutional status|
|86th|2002|Right to Education (Art. 21A)|
|101st|2016|GST|
|Exam trap|42nd Amendment is the single most-tested amendment — memorize its 3 Preamble additions cold||

## 7. Articles 🔴 (highest weight — 15 Qs)

|Article|Subject|
|---|---|
|14|Equality before law|
|19|6 freedoms (speech, assembly, association, movement, residence, profession)|
|21|Right to Life & Personal Liberty|
|21A|Right to Education|
|32|Constitutional Remedies|
|356|President's Rule (State Emergency)|
|368|Amendment procedure|
|370|Special status J&K (abrogated 2019)|
|371|Special provisions for certain states (incl. Mizoram — 371G)|
|372|Continuance of existing laws|
|Exam trap|**Article 371G** is Mizoram-specific — protects Mizo customary law/religion/social practices from Parliament override without State Assembly consent — very high priority for you specifically|

## 8. Judiciary 🟡

|Fact|Detail|
|---|---|
|SC established|28 January 1950|
|Judges|CJI + up to 33 other judges|
|Retirement age|SC: 65 years; High Court: 62 years|
|Judicial review|Power to strike down unconstitutional laws|
|Exam trap|India follows "Procedure Established by Law" (not US "Due Process of Law") — though judicial interpretation has blurred this since Maneka Gandhi case|

## 9. Panchayati Raj & Local Bodies 🟡

|Fact|Detail|
|---|---|
|Constitutional status|73rd Amendment (1992) — Panchayats; 74th — Municipalities|
|3-tier structure|Gram Panchayat → Panchayat Samiti (Block) → Zila Parishad (District)|
|Reserved seats|1/3rd seats reserved for women (some states 50%)|
|Exam trap|73rd/74th Amendments do NOT apply automatically to some tribal/hill areas — Mizoram has its own District Councils under **Sixth Schedule**, not standard Panchayati Raj|

## 10. Governor & State Government 🟡

|Fact|Detail|
|---|---|
|Appointment|By President; holds office "during pleasure of President"|
|Term|5 years (conventionally)|
|Discretionary powers|Reserving bills for President, recommending President's Rule|
|Exam trap|Governor can be common to 2+ states (one Governor for multiple states is allowed)|

## 11. Schedules of Constitution 🟡

|Schedule|Covers|
|---|---|
|1st|States & UTs list|
|2nd|Salaries of President, Governors, Judges, etc.|
|5th|Administration of Scheduled Areas/Tribes|
|6th|Administration of tribal areas — Assam, Meghalaya, Tripura, **Mizoram**|
|7th|Union/State/Concurrent Lists|
|8th|22 official languages (incl. Mizo? — verify: Mizo is NOT in 8th Schedule currently)|
|9th|Laws protected from judicial review|
|10th|Anti-defection law|
|Exam trap|**6th Schedule directly governs Mizoram's autonomous district councils** — high personal relevance, cross-link to Mizo History notes|

## 12. Writs 🟡

|Writ|Meaning|
|---|---|
|Habeas Corpus|"To have the body" — against illegal detention|
|Mandamus|"We command" — directs public official to perform duty|
|Prohibition|Higher court stops lower court from exceeding jurisdiction|
|Certiorari|Quashes an order already passed by lower authority|
|Quo Warranto|"By what authority" — challenges holding of public office|
|Exam trap|Prohibition = preventive (before order); Certiorari = corrective (after order) — most repeated writ confusion|

## 13. Emergency Provisions 🟡

|Type|Article|Grounds|
|---|---|---|
|National Emergency|352|War, external aggression, armed rebellion|
|State Emergency (President's Rule)|356|Failure of constitutional machinery in state|
|Financial Emergency|360|Threat to financial stability (never invoked so far)|
|Exam trap|National Emergency invoked 3 times (1962, 1971, 1975) — 1975 (internal disturbance ground, since changed to "armed rebellion" by 44th Amendment) is most tested||

## 14. Borrowed Features (Source Countries) 🟡

|Feature|Borrowed from|
|---|---|
|Fundamental Rights|USA|
|Directive Principles|Ireland|
|Parliamentary system|UK|
|Federal system with strong Centre|Canada|
|Concurrent List|Australia|
|Emergency provisions|Germany (Weimar Constitution)|
|Fundamental Duties|USSR (Russia)|
|Procedure Established by Law|Japan|
|Exam trap|Directive Principles ↔ Ireland is the single most repeated "borrowed feature" question|

## 15. Directive Principles & Duties 🟢

|Fact|Detail|
|---|---|
|DPSP location|Part IV, Articles 36–51|
|Fundamental Duties|Part IVA, Article 51A — added by 42nd Amendment, 1976|
|Number of duties|Originally 10, now 11 (86th Amendment added one on education)|
|Exam trap|DPSPs are non-justiciable (not enforceable in court) — commonly confused with Fundamental Rights which ARE justiciable|

## 16. Citizenship 🟢

|Fact|Detail|
|---|---|
|Governing Part|Part II, Articles 5–11|
|Single citizenship|India has only one citizenship (no dual state+national like USA)|
|Acquiring citizenship|Birth, descent, registration, naturalization|
|Exam trap|Constitution doesn't allow dual citizenship — "Overseas Citizen of India (OCI)" is NOT full citizenship|

## 17. Election Commission & Voting 🟢

|Fact|Detail|
|---|---|
|Established|Article 324|
|Structure|CEC + 2 Election Commissioners (multi-member since 1993)|
|Voting age|18 years (61st Amendment, 1989)|
|Exam trap|ECI conducts elections for President, VP, Parliament, State Legislatures — but NOT Panchayat/Municipal elections (those are State Election Commissions)|

## 18. UPSC & Constitutional Bodies 🟢

|Body|Article/Feature|
|---|---|
|UPSC|Article 315–323|
|CAG (Comptroller & Auditor General)|Article 148 — "guardian of public purse"|
|Attorney General|Article 76|
|Finance Commission|Article 280|
|Exam trap|CAG's report submitted to President, then examined by **Public Accounts Committee**, not directly by Parliament|
