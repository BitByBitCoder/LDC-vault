## Key Difference — FR vs DPSP

| Fundamental Rights | Directive Principles |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| Part III (Articles 12–35) | Part IV (Articles 36–51) |
| **Justiciable** — can go to court | **Not justiciable** — cannot enforce in court |
| Negative duties on State | Positive duties on State |
| Borrowed from **USA** | Borrowed from **Ireland** |
| Individual rights | Social/economic goals |

## 11 Fundamental Duties (Article 51A)

1. Abide by Constitution and respect National Flag & Anthem
2. Cherish noble ideals of freedom struggle
3. Uphold sovereignty, unity, integrity of India
4. Defend the country
5. Promote harmony and brotherhood
6. Preserve rich heritage and culture
7. Protect natural environment
8. Develop scientific temper and humanism
9. Safeguard public property
10. Strive for excellence in individual and collective activity
11. **Provide opportunities for education to children aged 6–14** (added by 86th Amendment)

## Exam Traps ⚠️
> DPSP = **NOT enforceable** in court — FR = **enforceable**
> Uniform Civil Code = **Article 44** — a DPSP, not a Fundamental Right
> Fundamental Duties = **11** (not 10 — 11th added by 86th Amendment)
> DPSP borrowed from **Ireland** — Duties borrowed from **USSR**
