The Godavari is ==India's second-longest river (after the Ganges), stretching 1,465 km==. Originating in the Western Ghats at Trimbakeshwar, Maharashtra, it flows eastward across the Deccan Plateau, crossing Maharashtra, Telangana, and Andhra Pradesh, before draining into the Bay of Bengal through a fertile delta in the Konaseema region. [[1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Godavari_River), [2](https://www.britannica.com/place/Godavari-River), [3](https://vajiramandravi.com/upsc-exam/godavari-river-system/)]

**Key Highlights:**

- **Cultural Significance:** Known as the _Dakshina Ganga_ (Southern Ganges), it is highly revered in Hindu scriptures and hosts the Nashik Kumbh Mela. [[1](https://www.drishtiias.com/daily-updates/daily-news-analysis/godavari-river-1), [2](https://simple.wikipedia.org/wiki/Godavari_River), [3](https://mapy.com/en/?source=osm&id=112735453), [4](https://unacademy.com/content/nta-ugc/study-material/business-and-general-awareness/origins-of-river-godavari/), [5](https://vajiramandravi.com/upsc-exam/godavari-river-system/)]

- **Basin Coverage:** Its basin spans about 312,813 km², covering roughly 10% of India's total land area across multiple states. [[1](https://vajiramandravi.com/upsc-exam/godavari-river-system/), [2](https://waterresources.karnataka.gov.in/new-page/Godavary%20River/en), [3](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lzL6Z__uQcg&t=11), [4](https://simple.wikipedia.org/wiki/Godavari_River), [5](https://unacademy.com/content/nta-ugc/study-material/business-and-general-awareness/origins-of-river-godavari/)]

- **Tributaries:** Key tributaries include the Pravara, Purna, Manjra, Pranahita, Indravati, and Sabari. [[1](https://waterresources.karnataka.gov.in/new-page/Godavary%20River/en)]

- **Economic Importance:** It is a lifeline for agriculture and power generation in South India, supported by major projects like the Sriram Sagar (Pochampadu) in Telangana and the Polavaram Project in Andhra Pradesh.
