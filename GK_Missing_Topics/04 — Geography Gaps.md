## Drainage Patterns — Complete Table

| Pattern | Shape | Example |
|---------|-------|---------|
| **Dendritic** | **Tree with branches** | Most common — Ganga plain |
| **Radial** | Streams flow outward from central hill | Amarkantak plateau |
| **Trellis** | Rectangular — main river + parallel tributaries | Himalayas |
| **Rectangular** | Right angle bends | Joints and faults in rocks |
| **Centripetal** | All streams flow toward central depression | Inland drainage — Rajasthan |
| **Parallel** | Streams flow parallel to each other | — |

## Exam Traps ⚠️
> Highest in India = **Kanchenjunga** — Highest in world = **Everest** (NOT in India)
> Western Ghats = **Sahyadri** — NOT Purvanchal or Aravalli
> EEZ = **200 nautical miles** — Territorial sea = **12 nautical miles**
> Sikkim = **does NOT border Myanmar** — often confused with Manipur/Nagaland
> Dendritic = **tree** pattern — Radial = streams flow FROM central point outward
> Chilika = **brackish** (largest) — Sambhar = **saltiest** — different facts
