## Types of Clauses — Complete

A clause = group of words with subject + verb.

| Type | Function | Introduced by | Example |
|------|---------|--------------|---------|
| **Main/Independent** | Complete sentence alone | — | She studies hard |
| **Noun Clause** | Acts as a noun (subject/object) | that, what, whether, how | I know **that he is right** |
| **Adjective Clause** | Modifies a noun | who, which, that, whose | The man **who came** |
| **Adverbial Clause** | Modifies a verb/adjective | when, where, because, since, as, if, though, until | He came **when I called** |

---

## Adverbial Clauses — Types (Most Tested)

| Sub-type | Conjunction | Example |
|---------|------------|---------|
| **Time** | when, while, after, before, until, since | "The farmer came home **when it was night**" |
| **Reason** | because, since, as | "I cannot attend **as I am unwell**" |
| **Condition** | if, unless, provided | "I will go **if it rains**" |
| **Contrast** | although, though, even though | "He passed **though he didn't study**" |
| **Purpose** | so that, in order that | "She studies **so that she may pass**" |
| **Result** | so...that, such...that | "It was **so dark that** I couldn't see" |
| **Place** | where, wherever | "Go **where** you like" |
| **Manner** | as, as if, as though | "Do **as** I say" |

## Noun Clauses — Quick Guide

| Position | Example |
|---------|---------|
| As subject | **That he will come** is certain |
| As object | I know **that he will come** |
| As complement | The truth is **that he is wrong** |
| After preposition | I am sorry **that you failed** |

> Introduced by: **that, what, whatever, who, whoever, whether, if, how, when, where, why**

> 💡 "I am sorry to observe **that you are very irregular**" = Noun Clause (object of observe)

## Adjective Clauses — Quick Guide

| Pronoun | Used For |
|---------|---------|
| **who** | People (subject) |
| **whom** | People (object) |
| **whose** | Possession |
| **which** | Things |
| **that** | People or things |

Example: "The book **that I bought** is good" — adjective clause modifying "book"

## Determiners — Complete List

A determiner comes before a noun and limits/specifies it.

| Type | Examples |
|------|---------|
| **Articles** | a, an, the |
| **Demonstratives** | this, that, these, those |
| **Possessives** | my, your, his, her, its, our, their |
| **Quantifiers** | some, any, many, much, few, little, all, both, each, every |
| **Numbers** | one, two, first, second |
| **Distributives** | each, every, either, neither |
| **Interrogatives** | which, what, whose |

### Key Determiners Tested

| Determiner | Use | Example |
|-----------|-----|---------|
| **Both** | Two things together | **Both** of them are intelligent |
| **Each** | Every one separately | **Each** student has a book |
| **Every** | All members of a group | **Every** dog has its day |
| **Either** | One of two | **Either** answer is correct |
| **Neither** | Not one of two | **Neither** of them came |
| **All** | The whole number | **All** students passed |
| **Some** | An unspecified amount | **Some** boys are lazy |

## Confusable Words (Appeared in Papers)

| Words | Difference |
|-------|-----------|
| **adept** vs adapt vs adopt | Adept = skilled at; Adapt = adjust; Adopt = take on |
| **site** vs sight vs cite | Site = location; Sight = vision; Cite = quote |
| **elicit** vs illicit | Elicit = to draw out/evoke; Illicit = illegal |
| **ceiling** vs sealing | Ceiling = top of room; Sealing = closing |
| **stationary** vs stationery | Stationary = not moving; Stationery = writing materials |
| **principal** vs principle | Principal = head/main; Principle = rule/belief |
| **affect** vs effect | Affect = verb (to influence); Effect = noun (result) |
| **complement** vs compliment | Complement = complete; Compliment = praise |
| **farther** vs further | Farther = physical distance; Further = extent/degree |
| **desert** vs dessert | Desert = dry land; Dessert = sweet course |

## Exam Traps ⚠️
> "when it was night" = **Adverbial** (time) — NOT noun or adjective clause
> "as I am unwell" = **Adverbial** (reason) — same as "because I am unwell"
> "that you are irregular" = **Noun clause** — object of "observe"
> **Both** = Determiner (before noun) — NOT pronoun when before noun
> **Adept** at = skilled; **Adapt** to = adjust; **Adopt** = take on — all different
> **Elicit** = draw out (verb); **Illicit** = illegal (adjective) — spelling matters
> **Site** = location for building; **Sight** = something you see; **Cite** = quote
