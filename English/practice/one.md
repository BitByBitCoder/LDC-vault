# Parts of Speech — Tagging Drill

Tag **every word** in each sentence with its part of speech before checking the answer.

Abbreviations: N (noun), Pro (pronoun), V (verb), Adj (adjective), Adv (adverb), Prep (preposition), Conj (conjunction), Det (determiner), Interj (interjection)

---

## 🟢 Easy (Level 1)

**1.** The dog barked loudly.

<details><summary>Answer</summary> The (Det) dog (N) barked (V) loudly (Adv) </details>

**2.** She quickly finished her homework.

<details><summary>Answer</summary> She (Pro) quickly (Adv) finished (V) her (Det) homework (N) </details>

**3.** Wow, that cake looks delicious!

<details><summary>Answer</summary> Wow (Interj) that (Det) cake (N) looks (V) delicious (Adj) </details>

**4.** He sat under the old tree.

<details><summary>Answer</summary> He (Pro) sat (V) under (Prep) the (Det) old (Adj) tree (N) </details>

**5.** They are playing football and cricket.

<details><summary>Answer</summary> They (Pro) are playing (V) football (N) and (Conj) cricket (N) </details>

## 🟡 Medium (Level 2)

**6.** Although it was raining, the children played outside happily.

<details><summary>Answer</summary> Although (Conj) it (Pro) was (V) raining (V) , the (Det) children (N) played (V) outside (Adv) happily (Adv) </details>

**7.** The manager gave a brief but clear explanation of the new policy.

<details><summary>Answer</summary> The (Det) manager (N) gave (V) a (Det) brief (Adj) but (Conj) clear (Adj) explanation (N) of (Prep) the (Det) new (Adj) policy (N) </details>

**8.** She always arrives early because she hates being late.

<details><summary>Answer</summary> She (Pro) always (Adv) arrives (V) early (Adv) because (Conj) she (Pro) hates (V) being (V) late (Adj) </details>

**9.** The heavy rain suddenly stopped near the old railway station.

<details><summary>Answer</summary> The (Det) heavy (Adj) rain (N) suddenly (Adv) stopped (V) near (Prep) the (Det) old (Adj) railway (N) station (N) </details>

**10.** He works hard, yet he rarely gets recognized.

<details><summary>Answer</summary> He (Pro) works (V) hard (Adv) , yet (Conj) he (Pro) rarely (Adv) gets (V) recognized (V) </details>

## 🔴 Hard — same word, different job (Level 3)

**11.** I need to water the plants before the water runs out.

<details><summary>Answer</summary> water #1 = V (to water) / water #2 = N (the water) </details>

**12.** She was too light to lift the light box, so she turned on the light instead.

<details><summary>Answer</summary> light #1 = Adj (not heavy) / light #2 = Adj (describing box) / light #3 = N (a lamp) </details>

**13.** Please back the car up so we can sit at the back of the hall.

<details><summary>Answer</summary> back #1 = V (to back up) / back #2 = N (the rear part) </details>

**14.** Round the corner, there's a round table where we round off our meetings.

<details><summary>Answer</summary> Round #1 = Prep (around) / round #2 = Adj (shape) / round #3 = V (to finish/complete) </details>

**15.** She is well aware that the well ran dry, but she took it well.

<details><summary>Answer</summary> well #1 = Adv (fully/clearly) / well #2 = N (water source) / well #3 = Adv (calmly/gracefully) </details>

## 🔴 Hard — full sentence, exam-style (Level 3)

**16.** Despite the sudden increase in prices, the shopkeeper refused to reduce his profit margin.

<details><summary>Answer</summary> Despite (Prep) the (Det) sudden (Adj) increase (N) in (Prep) prices (N) , the (Det) shopkeeper (N) refused (V) to reduce (V) his (Det) profit (N) margin (N) </details>

**17.** Either you complete the report today, or you will face serious consequences tomorrow.

<details><summary>Answer</summary> Either (Conj) you (Pro) complete (V) the (Det) report (N) today (Adv) , or (Conj) you (Pro) will face (V) serious (Adj) consequences (N) tomorrow (Adv) </details>

**18.** The committee, which met yesterday, has not yet announced its final decision.

<details><summary>Answer</summary> The (Det) committee (N) , which (Pro) met (V) yesterday (Adv) , has announced (V) not (Adv) yet (Adv) its (Det) final (Adj) decision (N) </details>

**19.** Ouch! I accidentally cut myself while chopping the vegetables quickly.

<details><summary>Answer</summary> Ouch (Interj) I (Pro) accidentally (Adv) cut (V) myself (Pro) while (Conj) chopping (V) the (Det) vegetables (N) quickly (Adv) </details>

**20.** Neither the manager nor the staff were informed about the sudden change in schedule.

<details><summary>Answer</summary> Neither (Conj) the (Det) manager (N) nor (Conj) the (Det) staff (N) were informed (V) about (Prep) the (Det) sudden (Adj) change (N) in (Prep) schedule (N) </details>
