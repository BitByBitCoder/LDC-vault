Here's the full picture — the 8 core categories, plus the subcategories exams love to test that most beginners miss.

## The 8 Parts of Speech (traditional system — matches most exam answer keys)

### 1. Noun

Types you'll be tested on:

- **Common** — ||dog, city||
- **Proper** — ||Mizoram, Ram||
- **Abstract** — ||honesty, happiness (can't touch it)||
- **Collective** — ||team, herd, committee||
- **Countable/Uncountable** — ||books (countable) vs. water (uncountable)||
- **Material** — ||gold, wood||

### 2. Pronoun

Often missed subtypes:

- **Personal** — ||I, you, he, she, it, they||
- **Possessive** — ||mine, yours, his, hers||
- **Reflexive** — ||myself, himself, themselves||
- **Demonstrative** — ||this, that, these, those||
- **Relative** — || who, which, that (connects clauses: "the man who came") ||
- **Interrogative** — ||who, what, which (in questions)||
- **Indefinite** — ||someone, anybody, everything, none||

### 3. Verb

Most-missed distinction:

- **Main/Action verb** — || run, eat, write||
- **Linking verb** — ||is, am, are, was, seem, become (links subject to a description, no action)||
- **Auxiliary/Helping verb** —|| is, has, will, must, can, should (helps the main verb)||
- **Transitive** || (needs an object: "she ate  rice") vs. Intransitive(no object: "she slept")||
- **Modal verbs** — ||can, could, may, might, must, shall, should, will, would (huge exam topic on their own)||

### 4. Adjective

Subtypes commonly tested:

- **Descriptive** — ||tall, blue, difficult||
- **Quantitative** — ||some, few, many, much||
- **Demonstrative** — ||this, that (when before a noun — overlaps with Determiner debate)||
- **Possessive** — ||my, your, his (also overlaps with Determiner)||
- **Degrees of comparison** —|| big → bigger → biggest (Positive/Comparative/Superlative — very high-frequency exam topic, often tested as its own section)||

### 5. Adverb

Types:

- **Manner** — ||quickly, well, loudly (how)||
- **Time** — ||now, yesterday, soon (when)||
- **Place** — ||here, there, outside (where)||
- **Frequency** — always, often, never (how often)
- **Degree** — very, too, quite, almost (how much)
- **Interrogative** — why, where, how (in questions)

### 6. Preposition

Missed forms:

- **Simple** — in, on, at, by
- **Compound** — into, onto, within
- **Phrasal (group prepositions)** — in front of, in spite of, because of, according to — **very commonly tested**, easy to miss since they're multi-word
- Prepositions of time (at, on, in), place (at, on, in), and direction (to, into, onto) each have their own confusing rule sets

### 7. Conjunction

Types:

- **Coordinating** — and, but, or, so, yet (FANBOYS) — joins equal things
- **Subordinating** — because, although, since, if, when — joins a dependent clause
- **Correlative** — either...or, neither...nor, both...and, not only...but also — **frequently tested and frequently missed**

### 8. Interjection

- Wow, Alas, Oh, Ouch, Hurrah — usually only 1–2 easy marks, low priority

---

## The commonly-added 9th category (know it, but check your exam key)

### 9. Determiner

Splits off from Adjective/Pronoun in modern grammar:

- **Articles** — a, an, the (also its own full exam topic — you already have this in your list)
- **Demonstrative** — this, that, these, those
- **Possessive** — my, your, our
- **Quantifiers** — some, any, few, much, many, all, every
- **Numbers** — one, first, three

---

## What people usually miss (the exam traps)

1. **Modal verbs** as their own drilling topic, not just "verbs"
2. **Phrasal/compound prepositions** (in spite of, on behalf of) — these get tested a lot and are easy to overlook
3. **Correlative conjunctions** (either...or) — often skipped because they "look like" two separate conjunctions
4. **Degrees of comparison** for adjectives — usually appears as its own exam question type, not folded into "adjective"
5. **Linking vs. action verbs** — a classic trap sentence: "She looks **tired**" (linking + adjective) vs. "She **looks** at him" (action verb)
