## Essay Writing — Strategy

### Structure (Always follow this)
```
Paragraph 1 — INTRODUCTION (3–4 sentences)
- Define the topic
- State its importance
- End with thesis statement

Paragraph 2 — POINT 1 (4–5 sentences)
- One main idea
- Explanation
- Example

Paragraph 3 — POINT 2 (4–5 sentences)
- Second main idea
- Explanation
- Example

Paragraph 4 — POINT 3 (4–5 sentences)
- Third main idea
- Explanation
- Example

Paragraph 5 — CONCLUSION (3–4 sentences)
- Summarize
- Final opinion
- Call to action or closing thought
```

### Common Essay Topics in LDC Papers
- Environment / Climate Change
- Digital India / Technology
- Education system
- Women empowerment
- Youth and future of India
- Drug addiction / Social evils
- COVID-19 impact
- Importance of discipline
- Sports and national development
- Corruption in India

### Useful Connecting Words
| Function | Words |
|----------|-------|
| Addition | Furthermore, Moreover, In addition, Besides |
| Contrast | However, Nevertheless, On the other hand, Although |
| Cause | Because, Since, As, Due to |
| Effect | Therefore, Thus, Hence, As a result |
| Example | For instance, For example, Such as |
| Conclusion | In conclusion, To summarize, Finally, In short |

### What Examiners Look For
1. Clear introduction and conclusion
2. Organized paragraphs (one idea per paragraph)
3. Correct grammar and spelling
4. Relevant content
5. Appropriate length (300–400 words minimum)
6. Vocabulary range — use synonyms, avoid repeating same word

## Comprehension — Strategy

### Step by Step
```
Step 1: Read ALL questions first (before reading passage)
Step 2: Read the passage once quickly for general idea
Step 3: Read passage again — underline answers to questions
Step 4: Write answers in your OWN words (not copy-paste from passage)
Step 5: Keep answers concise — 1–2 sentences per question
```

### Answer Format
- Answer in complete sentences
- Start answer with the question keyword
- Don't copy sentences word for word — paraphrase
- Stick to what the passage says — don't add outside knowledge

### Vocabulary in Context Questions
- "The word X in paragraph 2 means..."
- Look at surrounding sentences for clues
- Choose answer that fits the context

## Letter Writing — Two Types

### Formal Letter (to officer/authority)
```
[Your address]
[Date]

To,
The [Designation],
[Office name],
[Address]

Subject: [Brief subject line]

Sir/Madam,

[Body — state the matter clearly]

Yours faithfully,
[Your name]
```

### Informal Letter (to friend/family)
```
[Your address]
[Date]

Dear [Name],

[Opening — how are you]
[Body — main content]
[Closing — regards]

Your friend/Yours lovingly,
[Your name]
```

## Précis Writing

**Précis** = Summarize a passage in 1/3 of original length

### Steps
1. Read passage fully
2. Identify main ideas — ignore examples and repetitions
3. Write in your own words
4. Length = 1/3 of original
5. Give a title
6. Write in third person (he/she/they — NOT I/we)
7. Use past tense

---

## Grammar Rules for Writing

| Rule | Correct |
|------|---------|
| Paragraph indent | First line of each paragraph slightly inward |
| No abbreviations | Write "cannot" not "can't" in formal |
| Subject-verb agreement | Check every sentence |
| Tense consistency | Don't switch tense mid-essay |
| Punctuation | Full stop, comma, question mark — use correctly |

## Quick Marks Strategy

**If time is short in exam:**
- Essay: Write 3 proper paragraphs (intro + 2 body + conclusion) = still get 12-14/20
- Comprehension: Answer all questions even if short — partial marks given
- Never leave Part B blank — even poor answers get some marks
- Spelling mistakes reduce marks — check while writing

## Exam Traps ⚠️
> Write essay in **paragraphs** — NOT as a list
> Comprehension answers in **own words** — NOT copied from passage
> Formal letter — **Yours faithfully** (when you don't know the name) / **Yours sincerely** (when you know the name)
> Précis — **1/3 length**, third person, past tense
> Part B has NO negative marking — attempt everything
