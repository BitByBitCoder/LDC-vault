## Questions From Your Papers

| Wrong Sentence | Correct Sentence | Error |
|----------------|-----------------|-------|
| She **don't** like coffee | She **doesn't** like coffee | She = singular → doesn't |
| She **doesn't likes** coffee | She **doesn't like** coffee | After does/doesn't → base verb |
| **Hardly had finished** he when bell rang | **Hardly had he finished** when bell rang | Inversion after negative adverb |
| He **hardly had** finished | **Hardly had he** finished | Same — inversion |
| The crowd left **one after one** | left **one by one** | Fixed phrase |
| He climbed up the tree **and** plucked | **Climbing up** the tree, he plucked | Sentence combining |

## Most Common Error Types

### Error 1 — Do/Does/Did + Base Verb (NOT V1+s)
```
❌ She doesn't likes coffee.
✅ She doesn't like coffee.

❌ He doesn't goes to school.
✅ He doesn't go to school.

Rule: After do/does/did → ALWAYS base verb (V1)
```

### Error 2 — Inversion After Negative Adverbs
```
Negative adverbs: Hardly, Scarcely, Never, Rarely, Seldom, No sooner, Not only

Rule: Negative adverb at start → Auxiliary + Subject + Verb (question word order)

❌ Hardly he had finished when the bell rang.
✅ Hardly had he finished when the bell rang.

❌ Never I have seen such beauty.
✅ Never have I seen such beauty.

❌ Seldom she goes out at night.
✅ Seldom does she go out at night.
```

### Error 3 — Misuse of "as...as" Comparisons
```
❌ He is as meek as a lamb. (actually this is CORRECT)
As ___ as + positive adjective → correct form
```

### Error 4 — Double Negatives
```
❌ He doesn't know nothing.
✅ He doesn't know anything.
✅ He knows nothing.
```

### Error 5 — Wrong Conjunction
```
❌ She studies hard, lest she will fail.
✅ She studies hard, lest she fail. (lest → base verb, no will)

❌ He succeeded because his hard work.
✅ He succeeded because of his hard work. (because = conjunction + clause; because of = preposition + noun)
```

### Error 6 — Redundancy (Extra Words)
```
❌ He climbed up the tree. (climb already means going up)
✅ He climbed the tree.
(But in your paper "climbed up the tree" appeared as-is — check context)
```

### Error 7 — Incorrect Participle
```
❌ Climbing up the tree, mangoes were plucked.
✅ Climbing up the tree, he plucked some mangoes.
(Dangling participle — subject of main clause must be the one climbing)
```

## Fixed Phrases (Cannot Be Changed)

| Correct | Wrong |
|---------|-------|
| one **by** one | one after one |
| **in** the nick of time | at the nick of time |
| tit **for** tat | tit to tat |
| thick and **thin** | thick and thin ✅ (correct) |
| **in** a body | at a body |

---

## Sentence Combining — Simple Sentence

```
Complex → Simple:
"He climbed up the tree and plucked some mangoes"
= "Climbing up the tree, he plucked some mangoes"
(V+ing = simultaneous/prior action by same subject)
```

## Exam Traps ⚠️
> After **doesn't/don't/didn't** → ALWAYS base verb (no s/es)
> **Hardly had he** (inversion) → NOT hardly he had
> **Because of** + noun/phrase; **Because** + full clause
> **Lest** → base verb or should (NEVER will/would)
> Inversion after: Hardly, Never, Seldom, Rarely, No sooner, Not only, Scarcely
