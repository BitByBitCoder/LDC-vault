Since this is General English for the LDC exam, the smart order follows dependency (what builds on what) plus your frequency-data approach — get the topics that unlock others first, and the highest-yield "sitting duck" topics (fixed rules, memorizable, low ambiguity) early for quick marks.

### Recommended order

**1. Parts of Speech** ✅ (you're here)  
Foundation for everything below.

**2. Articles (a/an/the)**  
Small topic, fixed rules, quick to master, and shows up constantly in Sentence Correction.

**3. Tenses**  
Second foundational pillar after parts of speech — Active/Passive, Direct/Indirect, and Sentence Correction all lean on this heavily.

**4. Prepositions**  
High-frequency error-based questions in most exams, fixed set of rules/idiomatic pairs, good ROI.

**5. Active & Passive Voice**  
Directly built from Tenses — do this right after so the tense-transformation rules are fresh.

**6. Direct & Indirect Speech**  
Also builds on Tenses (backshift rules) — pairs naturally with #5.

**7. Conjunctions & Connectors**  
Builds on Parts of Speech; needed for Sentence Correction and Comprehension.

**8. Sentence Correction**  
This is the "integration" topic — it tests everything above at once. Do it only after 1–7 are solid, since every error type it tests comes from those rules.

**9. Synonyms & Antonyms**  
Pure vocabulary — no dependency on grammar, so it can run in parallel with anything above as daily flashcard/spaced-repetition work rather than a dedicated block.

**10. One Word Substitution**  
Same as above — vocabulary-driven, good for parallel spaced repetition.

**11. Idioms & Phrases**  
Same category — memorization-heavy, run alongside vocab work.

**12. Spelling**  
Fixed list of commonly-confused words (exam-specific) — quick drilling topic, can slot in anywhere.

**13. Comprehension**  
Needs vocabulary (9–11) and grammar (1–8) both in place — it's applied reading, not new rules.

**14. Essay Writing**  
Last, since it depends on everything — vocabulary, grammar, and structure all come together here.

---

**Practical suggestion:** Treat vocab topics (Synonyms, Antonyms, One Word Substitution, Idioms, Spelling) as a **daily parallel track** using your spaced-repetition/Obsidian system, while working through the grammar topics (1–8) sequentially as your **main block**.
