## The Core Rule

```
DIRECT:   He said, "I am happy."
INDIRECT: He said that he was happy.
```

**3 things change in Indirect Speech:**
1. **Reporting verb** (said, told, asked, etc.)
2. **Pronouns** (I → he/she, we → they, you → he/she/they)
3. **Tense** (backshift — present → past, past → past perfect)

---

## Tense Backshift Table

| Direct (inside quotes) | Indirect |
|-----------------------|---------|
| Simple Present (am/is/are) | Simple Past (was/were) |
| Present Continuous (is going) | Past Continuous (was going) |
| Present Perfect (has gone) | Past Perfect (had gone) |
| Simple Past (went) | Past Perfect (had gone) |
| Will | Would |
| Can | Could |
| May | Might |
| Must | Had to |

## Types of Indirect Speech

### Statements (said/told)
```
Direct:   He said, "I am tired."
Indirect: He said that he was tired.

Direct:   She said to me, "You are wrong."
Indirect: She told me that I was wrong.
```
> ⚠️ said to + person → **told** + person (no "to")

### Questions (asked/enquired)

**Yes/No Questions → if/whether**
```
Direct:   He said, "Are you coming?"
Indirect: He asked if/whether I was coming.
```

**Wh- Questions → same wh- word**
```
Direct:   She said, "Where do you live?"
Indirect: She asked where I lived.
```

### Commands/Requests (told/asked/ordered/requested)
```
Direct:   He said, "Close the door."
Indirect: He told me to close the door.

Direct:   She said, "Please help me."
Indirect: She requested me to help her.

Direct:   He said, "Don't go there."
Indirect: He told me not to go there.
```

---

## When Tense Does NOT Change

If the reported speech contains:
- **Universal truth** — "The earth revolves around the sun" → stays present
- **Habitual fact** — "Most people have breakfast daily" → can stay present

> This is why in your paper: "Most people **have** breakfast daily" — the answer was: "most people **had** breakfast daily" OR "most people **have** breakfast daily" (both can be acceptable — universal habit)

---

## Exam Traps ⚠️
> **said to** + person → changes to **told** in indirect (never "said to" in indirect)
> Questions in indirect → **no question mark**, normal word order
> "Will" → **would**; "Can" → **could**; "May" → **might** in indirect
> Commands → **to + V1** (positive) / **not to + V1** (negative)
> Universal truths → tense does NOT backshift
