# 03 — Active & Passive Voice
[[📝 English — INDEX|← Back to Index]]

---

## Questions From Your Papers

| Active | Passive (Answer) |
|--------|-----------------|
| They are painting the house | **The house is being painted by them** |
| The tailor was stitching my dress | **My dress was being stitched by the tailor** |
| Are the poor not being helped by the rich? | **Are the rich not helping the poor?** |
| The book had been read by the time bell rang | The book **had been read** (already passive) |
| Call him in | **Let him be called in** |

---

## The Formula

```
ACTIVE:  Subject + Verb + Object
PASSIVE: Object + be(correct form) + V3 + by + Subject
```

---

## Passive Formula by Tense

| Tense | Active | Passive |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Simple Present | She **writes** a letter | A letter **is written** by her |
| Present Continuous | She **is writing** a letter | A letter **is being written** by her |
| Present Perfect | She **has written** a letter | A letter **has been written** by her |
| Simple Past | She **wrote** a letter | A letter **was written** by her |
| Past Continuous | She **was writing** a letter | A letter **was being written** by her |
| Past Perfect | She **had written** a letter | A letter **had been written** by her |
| Simple Future | She **will write** a letter | A letter **will be written** by her |
| Modal | She **can write** a letter | A letter **can be written** by her |

---

## Special Cases (From Your Papers)

### Imperative Sentences (Commands)
```
Active:  Call him in.
Passive: Let him be called in.

Active:  Open the door.
Passive: Let the door be opened.

Active:  Help the poor.
Passive: Let the poor be helped.
```

### Interrogative (Questions)
```
Active:  Are the rich helping the poor?
Passive: Are the poor being helped by the rich?

Active:  Did she write the letter?
Passive: Was the letter written by her?
```

### Negative Passive
```
Active:  He is not liked by anyone.
Passive: None likes him. / Nobody likes him.
(Note: "None likes" — singular verb with none)
```

---

## Key Rules

**Rule 1:** The **object** of active becomes the **subject** of passive

**Rule 2:** The **subject** of active becomes the **agent** (by + subject) in passive — can be omitted if unknown

**Rule 3:** The **verb** changes to — be (correct form) + V3

**Rule 4:** Pronouns change:
| Active (Subject) | Passive (Object) |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| I | me |
| We | us |
| He | him |
| She | her |
| They | them |

---

## When "by" is omitted
When the agent is unknown, unimportant, or obvious:
- "The thief was caught" (no need to say by police — obvious)
- "English is spoken here" (no agent needed)

## Exam Traps ⚠️
> Present Continuous passive = **is being** painted — NOT is been / was being
> Past Continuous passive = **was being** stitched — NOT was been / is being
> Imperative passive = **Let + object + be + V3** — NOT "You must..."
> "None likes" — None takes **singular** verb
> Tense of "be" must match the original tense — don't change the tense
