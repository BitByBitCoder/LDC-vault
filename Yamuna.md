From a competitive exam perspective, the **Ganga River System** is the most frequently tested geographical topic in India. It is treated as the country's lifeline and its largest river basin, draining roughly 26% of India's landmass.

When examiners frame questions about the Ganga, they focus heavily on three core areas: **its complex origin, its exact tributaries, and its statutory/national facts.**
![](images%201.jpeg)
### 1. The Core Origin Matrix (Panch Prayag)

The Ganga does not start as a single stream. It is formed by the confluence of multiple glacial rivers in Uttarakhand. You must memorize the specific river junctions (**Prayags**) because they are high-yield static GK options:

- **Vishnuprayag:** Alaknanda + Dhauliganga
    
- **Nandaprayag:** Alaknanda + Nandakini
    
- **Karnaprayag:** Alaknanda + Pindar River
    
- **Rudraprayag:** Alaknanda + Mandakini
    
- **Devprayag:** **Alaknanda + Bhagirathi** (This is where the river officially takes the hming **_Ganga_**).
    

### 📊 Tributaries Breakdown (Left vs. Right Bank)

Questions will explicitly ask: _"Which of the following is NOT a left-bank tributary of Ganga?"_ Keep this alignment completely separated in your notes:

|**Left-Bank Tributaries (Hmar lam peng)**|**Right-Bank Tributaries (Chhim lam peng)**|
|---|---|
|**Ramganga** (First major tributary to join)|**Yamuna** (Largest and longest tributary overall)|

|**Gandak** & **Burhi Gandak**|**Punpun**|
|**Kosi** (Famous as the _"Sorrow of Bihar"_)||

### 📌 Critical Statutory & General Facts (Exam One-Liners)

- **Total Length:** **2,525 km** (Longest river inside India).
    
- **National Status:** Declared the **National River of India** in November 2008.
    
- **National Aquatic Animal:** The **Ganges River Dolphin** (_Platanista gangetica_).
    
- **National Waterway 1 (NW-1):** Runs from **Prayagraj (UP) to Haldia (WB)** along the Ganga-Bhagirathi-Hooghly river system. It is the longest waterway in India.
    
- **International Course:** It splits in West Bengal into the **Hooghly** (flows through Kolkata) and the main stream which enters Bangladesh under the name **Padma River**. It then joins the Brahmaputra (_Jamuna_) to form the **Sunderbans Delta** (world's largest delta) before entering the Bay of Bengal.
