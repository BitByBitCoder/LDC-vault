# 📜 Constitution

> ## 💡 Big Idea
A Constitution is the **highest set of rules** that defines **how a country is governed, limits government power, and protects the rights of its people.**

---

# 🤔 Why does a Constitution exist?

Imagine there is no Constitution.

Ask yourself:

- Who makes laws?
- Who becomes Prime Minister?
- Who has more power—the President or Parliament?
- What if the government becomes unfair?
- What rights do citizens have?
- Who decides if a law is unconstitutional?

Without a Constitution, every government could make its own rules.

➡️ A Constitution provides stability and prevents abuse of power.

---

# ❓What problem does it solve?

A Constitution solves several problems:

- Prevents unlimited government power.
- Protects citizens' rights.
- Defines the powers of each institution.
- Resolves conflicts between institutions.
- Creates a stable system of government.

# ⚖ Compare

## Constitution vs Ordinary Law

| Constitution | Ordinary Law |
|--------------|--------------|
| Highest law | Must follow the Constitution |
| Difficult to amend | Easier to amend |
| Creates government | Government makes laws under it |

# 🌎 Real Examples

- A law violates Fundamental Rights.
    → Supreme Court can strike it down.

- Parliament wants to change the Constitution.
    → It must follow the amendment procedure.

- Two states have a dispute.
    → The Constitution provides the framework for resolving it.

# 🚨 Common Misconceptions

❌ Constitution only contains Fundamental Rights.

✅ It defines the entire system of government.

---

❌ Constitution controls only citizens.

✅ It mainly controls the government.

---

❌ Parliament is more powerful than the Constitution.

✅ Parliament derives its powers from the Constitution.

# 📝 One-Sentence Summary

The Constitution is the highest law that creates the government, limits its power, protects citizens, and provides the rules that keep democracy functioning.
