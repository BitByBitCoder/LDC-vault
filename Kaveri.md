The **Kaveri River** (often spelled _Cauvery_) is one of the most sacred and geopolitically significant rivers of Peninsular India. Often referred to as the **"Ganga of the South"** due to its spiritual significance and perennial-like flow, it is a high-yield topic for competitive exams.

Here is the complete geographical breakdown of the Kaveri River system.

### 1. Origin & Route

- **Source:** **Talakaveri** in the **Brahmagiri Range** of the Western Ghats, located in the Kodagu (Coorg) district of Karnataka.
    
- **Elevation:** Approximately 1,341 meters above sea level.
    
- **Length:** Around 800 km.
    
- **Drainage Basin:** Covers an area of roughly 81,155 sq. km across **Karnataka (34%)**, **Tamil Nadu (56%)**, **Kerala (3%)**, and the Union Territory of **Puducherry**.
    
- **Outflow:** It empties into the **Bay of Bengal** at Poompuhar in the Mayiladuthurai district of Tamil Nadu.

### 3. Critical Exam Highlights (Static GK)

#### 🔹 The "Dual Monsoon" Phenomenon (Why it rarely runs dry)

Unlike other peninsular rivers (like the Godavari or Krishna) which dry up significantly in the summer, the Kaveri maintains a relatively constant flow year-round. This is due to its unique location:

- **Upper Catchment (Karnataka):** Receives rainfall from the **Southwest Monsoon** (June to September).
    
- **Lower Catchment (Tamil Nadu):** Receives rainfall from the **Northeast Monsoon** (October to December).
    

#### 🔹 Important Dams & Projects

Referencing the map above, you can locate two highly contested reservoirs:

- **Krishna Raja Sagara (KRS) Dam:** Located in Karnataka, just below the junction of the Hemavati and Lakshmantirtha tributaries.
    
- **Mettur Dam:** Located in Tamil Nadu, right where the river enters the plains.
    
- **Grand Anicut (Kallanai):** Built by King Karikala Chola in the 2nd century AD, it is one of the oldest water-regulatory structures in the world still in use.
    

#### 🔹 Famous Waterfalls & Islands

- **Shivasamudram Falls:** The river branches to create the island of Shivasamudram in Karnataka, plunging down to form famous twin waterfalls (Gaganachukki and Bharachukki).
    
- **First Hydroelectric Power Station:** In 1902, India's first major hydroelectric project was commissioned here at Shivasamudram.
    
- **Hogenakkal Falls:** Often called the "Niagara of India," located on the border of Karnataka and Tamil Nadu.
