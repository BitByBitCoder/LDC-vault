That's actually a very common issue.

From our recent math practice, your understanding of concepts like fractions, HCF, percentages, and averages seems to be improving. The mistakes often happen during **basic calculations**, not because you don't understand the topic.

For example:

- 8 + 7 → you accidentally write 14 instead of 15
    
- 6 × 8 → you hesitate or use the wrong table
    
- While solving a long problem, you miss a sign (`+`, `−`, `×`, `÷`)
    

These are **calculation accuracy** problems.

### How to Fix It

#### 1. Slow Down for 2 Weeks

Don't focus on speed.

When you see:

```
27 + 38
```

Actually say to yourself:

```
7 + 8 = 15
Write 5, carry 1
2 + 3 + 1 = 6
Answer = 65
```

Train accuracy first.

---

#### 2. Practice Mental Arithmetic Daily

Spend 10 minutes a day on questions like:

```
37 + 48
65 - 29
8 × 7
9 × 6
84 ÷ 7
```

Do 20–30 questions.

---

#### 3. Master Tables

For competitive exams, know:

- 2–20 tables perfectly
    
- Squares up to 30²
    
- Cubes up to 20³
    

If multiplication is weak, many topics become harder.

---

#### 4. Check Every Answer Once

After solving:

```
47 + 36 = 83
```

Quickly verify:

```
83 - 36 = 47
```

This catches many careless mistakes.

---

#### 5. Separate "Math Learning" from "Calculation Training"

You should spend time on both:

**Concepts**

- Fractions
    
- Percentages
    
- Average
    
- Ratio
    
- Algebra
    

**Calculation Skills**

- Addition
    
- Subtraction
    
- Multiplication
    
- Division
    
- Tables
    

Many students study only concepts and neglect calculation practice.

---
