For **64 and 96**:

64 = 2⁶

96 = 2⁵ × 3

The common prime factor is **2**.

Take the **smaller power**:

2⁵ = 32

✅ **HCF = 32**

You can also see it as:

64 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2

96 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 3

There are **five common 2's**, so:

2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 = 32

✅ HCF = 32

You're starting to get the idea: for HCF with prime factorization, **keep only the common primes and use the smallest exponent**.
