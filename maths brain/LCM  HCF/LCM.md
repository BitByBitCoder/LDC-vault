
# LCM (Least Common Multiple)

The **LCM** is the **smallest number that is a multiple of all the given numbers**.

---

## Example 1: LCM of 4 and 6

Multiples of 4:

4, 8, **12**, 16, 20, ...

Multiples of 6:

6, **12**, 18, 24, ...

The smallest common multiple is **12**.

**LCM = 12**

---

## Example 2: Using Prime Factorization

Find the LCM of 24 and 36.

24 = 2³ × 3

36 = 2² × 3²

Take each prime factor with the **largest power**:

2³ × 3²

= 8 × 9

= **72**

So:

**LCM(24, 36) = 72**

---

## Example 3: LCM of 12 and 18

12 = 2² × 3

18 = 2 × 3²

Take the largest power of each prime:

2² × 3²

= 4 × 9

= **36**

So:

**LCM = 36**

---

## Quick Relationship

For two numbers:

LCM × HCF = Product of the numbers

Example:

12 and 18

HCF = 6

LCM = (12 × 18) ÷ 6

= 216 ÷ 6

= **36**

---

## Practice

Find the LCM of:

1. 6 and 8
    
2. 12 and 15
    
3. 18 and 24
    
4. 84 and 126
    
5. 11 and 29
    

Answers:

1. 24
    
2. 60
    
3. 72
    
4. 252
    
5. 319
    

A good rule to remember:

- **HCF → take common prime factors with the smallest powers.**
    
- **LCM → take all prime factors with the largest powers.**
