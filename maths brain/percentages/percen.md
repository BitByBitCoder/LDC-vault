


For LDC-level percentage questions, here's the complete pattern map:

---

## Core Formula (Everything derives from this)

```
Percentage = (Part / Whole) × 100
```

---

## The 8 Question Types That Actually Appear

**1. X% of Y** → Direct. Just (X/100) × Y

**2. What % is X of Y?** → (X/Y) × 100

**3. A is X% more/less than B — what % is B more/less than A?** → The classic trap. Formula:

- More: x/(100+x) × 100
- Less: x/(100-x) × 100

**4. Value after % increase/decrease** → Multiply directly: increase by 20% = × 1.2, decrease by 20% = × 0.8

**5. Successive % changes** → Formula: a + b + (ab/100) — don't add percentages directly

**6. % change (find the difference)** → (New - Old)/Old × 100

**7. Population/price increase problems** → P × (1 ± r/100)^n — but LDC rarely goes beyond n=2

**8. Fraction ↔ Percentage conversions** → Just memorize: 1/8 = 12.5%, 1/6 = 16.67%, 1/3 = 33.33%, 2/3 = 66.67%, 3/8 = 37.5%

## The One Trap That Kills Most People

**"A's salary is 25% more than B. By what % is B's salary less than A?"**

Wrong instinct: answer 25%. Wrong.

Correct:

```
A = B × 1.25
B = A / 1.25 = A × 0.8
B is less than A by 20%
Formula: x/(100+x) × 100 = 25/125 × 100 = 20%
```

Memorize this pattern cold. It appears in almost every paper.
