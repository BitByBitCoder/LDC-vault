

# Step 1: Train Crossing a Pole

Imagine a train is 180 m long.

```text
[========== TRAIN ==========]  Pole
```

To completely cross the pole:

- The front of the train reaches the pole.
    
- Then the entire train must pass it.
    

So the train must travel its **own length**.

### Formula

Distance = Length of Train

Time = Distance ÷ Speed

---

### Example 1

Train length = 120 m

Speed = 10 m/s

Time = 120 ÷ 10

= **12 seconds**

---

### Example 2

Train length = 240 m

Speed = 20 m/s

Time = 240 ÷ 20

= **12 seconds**

---

# Step 2: Speed Conversion

Exam questions often give speed in km/h.

### Must Memorize

\text{m/s}=\text{km/h}\times\frac{5}{18}

### Example

54 km/h

= 54 × 5/18

= 15 m/s

---

72 km/h

= 72 × 5/18

= 20 m/s

---

# Example 3

Train length = 180 m

Speed = 54 km/h

Convert speed:

54 × 5/18 = 15 m/s

Time

= 180 ÷ 15

= **12 seconds**

---

# Shortcut Pattern

Notice:

- 180 m at 15 m/s → 12 s
    
- 240 m at 20 m/s → 12 s
    
- 120 m at 10 m/s → 12 s
    

You are always doing:

Time = Length ÷ Speed
