Perfect. Before solving train questions, understand **what a train question is actually asking**.

# Think of a Train as a Moving Line

A train has a length.

Example:

```text
Front -------------------- Back
<------ 100 metres ------->
```

If the train is 100 m long, it is not a single point. It occupies 100 m of track.

This is why train questions are different from normal speed-distance questions.

---

# Example: Crossing a Pole

Imagine:

```text
Train                    Pole
===========>             |
```

The front of the train reaches the pole.

Has the train crossed the pole?

**No.**

The back of the train is still behind the pole.

---

Only when the entire train passes:

```text
                      =========>
                      Train
Pole |
```

has the train crossed the pole.

---

# How Far Did It Travel?

Suppose train length = 100 m.

From the moment the front reaches the pole until the back passes the pole, the train has travelled exactly **100 m**.

That is why:

Distance = Length of Train

---

# Example

Train length = 120 m

Speed = 10 m/s

Distance to cross pole = 120 m

Time = Distance ÷ Speed

= 120 ÷ 10

= **12 seconds**

---

# Crossing a Platform

Now imagine a platform.

```text
Train                Platform
===========>     [==========]
```

To completely cross the platform:

1. The train must enter the platform.
    
2. The train must travel through the whole platform.
    
3. The back of the train must leave the platform.
    

So the train travels:

Train length + Platform length

---

# Example

Train = 100 m

Platform = 50 m

Distance to cross

= 100 + 50

= 150 m

If speed = 10 m/s

Time

= 150 ÷ 10

= **15 seconds**

---

# Why Add the Lengths?

Think of it this way.

```text
Train = 100 m
Platform = 50 m
```

The front of the train must move:

- 50 m to cover the platform
    
- plus another 100 m so the back clears it
    

Total = 150 m

---

# Two Types You Must Know First

### Type 1: Train Crossing Pole

Distance = Train Length

Example:

Train = 200 m

Speed = 20 m/s

Time = 200 ÷ 20 = 10 s

---

### Type 2: Train Crossing Platform

Distance = Train Length + Platform Length

Example:

Train = 200 m

Platform = 100 m

Speed = 20 m/s

Time = (200 + 100) ÷ 20

= 300 ÷ 20

= 15 s
