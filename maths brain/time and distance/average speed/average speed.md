# Average Speed

Average Speed is one of the most important Time & Distance topics.

---

# What is Average Speed?

Average Speed tells us:

> "Overall speed for the entire journey."

### Formula
![[Screenshot 2026-06-06 at 11.48.20 PM.png]]
Average Speed=Total TimeTotal Distance​
---

# Example 1

A car travels:

- 60 km in 1 hour
    
- 40 km in 1 hour
    

Total Distance:

[  
60 + 40 = 100  
]

Total Time:

[  
1 + 1 = 2  
]

Average Speed:

[  
100/2 = 50  
]

✅ Answer = **50 km/hr**

---

# Important Point

**Do NOT simply average the speeds unless the times are equal.**

---

# Case 1: Equal Time

Suppose:

- First hour → 60 km/hr
    
- Second hour → 40 km/hr
    

Time is equal (1 hour each).

Average Speed:

[  
(60+40)/2  
]

[  
100/2=50  
]

✅ Answer = **50 km/hr**

### Rule

When **time is equal**:

\text{Average Speed}=\frac{S_1+S_2}{2}

---

# Case 2: Equal Distance

Suppose a car travels:

- 60 km at 60 km/hr
    
- 60 km at 40 km/hr
    

Distances are equal.

Can we do:

[  
(60+40)/2=50  
]

❌ Wrong!

Let's calculate.

---

Time for first 60 km:

[  
60/60=1  
]

Time for second 60 km:

[  
60/40=1.5  
]

Total Distance:

[  
60+60=120  
]

Total Time:

[  
1+1.5=2.5  
]

Average Speed:

[  
120/2.5=48  
]

✅ Answer = **48 km/hr**

---

# Shortcut for Equal Distances

When two equal distances are covered at speeds **a** and **b**:

![[Screenshot 2026-06-07 at 12.12.08 AM.png]]

---

### Example

Speeds:

- 60 km/hr
    
- 40 km/hr
    

Average Speed:

[  
\frac{2\times60\times40}{60+40}  
]

[  
\frac{4800}{100}  
]

[  
48  
]

✅ Answer = **48 km/hr**

---

# Easy Memory Trick

### Equal Time

```text
1 hr @ 60
1 hr @ 40
```

Just use:

[  
(S_1+S_2)/2  
]

---

### Equal Distance

```text
60 km @ 60
60 km @ 40
```

Use:

[  
2ab/(a+b)  
]

This is called the **harmonic mean shortcut**.

### Golden Rule

Always remember:

**Average Speed = Total Distance ÷ Total Time**

If you forget every shortcut, this formula will always give the correct answer. ✅
