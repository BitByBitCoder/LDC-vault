For competitive exams (SSC, Banking, Railways, State Exams, etc.), **Time, Speed and Distance (TSD)** is usually divided into these topics:

# 1. Basic Formula

- Speed
    
- Distance
    
- Time
    
- Unit conversion (m/s ↔ km/h)
    

### Must Know

\text{Speed}=\frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}}

---

# 2. Average Speed

Example:

- Go at 60 km/h
    
- Return at 40 km/h
    

Find average speed.

This is a very common exam question.

---

# 3. Relative Speed

When objects move:

- Same direction
    
- Opposite direction
    

Example:

Train A = 60 km/h

Train B = 40 km/h

Relative speed:

- Same direction = 60 − 40 = 20 km/h
    
- Opposite direction = 60 + 40 = 100 km/h
    

---

# 4. Trains

Most important TSD chapter.

Questions include:

- Train crossing a pole
    
- Train crossing a platform
    
- Train crossing another train
    
- Two trains moving opposite directions
    
- Two trains moving same direction
    

---

# 5. Boats and Streams

Terms:

- Upstream
    
- Downstream
    
- Stream speed
    

Formulas:

Downstream speed = Boat speed + Stream speed

Upstream speed = Boat speed − Stream speed

---

# 6. Circular Track Problems

Examples:

- Two runners on a circular track
    
- When do they meet?
    
- How many times do they cross?
    

Common in SSC and Railway exams.

---

# 7. Race Problems

Examples:

- A beats B by 50 m
    
- B beats C by 20 m
    

Find who wins and by how much.

---

# 8. Meeting Point Problems

Examples:

- Two cars start from different cities
    
- Find where they meet
    
- Find when they meet
    

---

# 9. Speed Change Problems

Example:

A train increases speed by 20%.

How much time is saved?

Very common in Banking exams.

---

# 10. Time and Distance Ratios

Example:

A:B speed ratio = 3:4

Distance same.

Find time ratio.

These are shortcut-heavy questions.

---

# 11. Unit Conversion

Must memorize:

1\text{ m/s}=\frac{18}{5}\text{ km/h}

and

1\text{ km/h}=\frac{5}{18}\text{ m/s}

---

# Order to Learn

1. Speed = Distance ÷ Time
    
2. Unit Conversion
    
3. Average Speed
    
4. Relative Speed
    
5. Trains
    
6. Boats & Streams
    
7. Circular Track
    
8. Race Problems
    
9. Speed-Time Ratios
    
10. Advanced Mixed Problems
    

If your goal is competitive exams and your math foundation is still developing, spend most of your time first on:

- Fractions
    
- Percentages
    
- Ratio & Proportion
    
- Average
    
- Time, Speed & Distance
    
- Time & Work
    

These six topics form a large portion of aptitude questions and make the advanced TSD problems much easier.
