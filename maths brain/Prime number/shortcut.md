# Fast Shortcut Method to Check Prime Numbers

For competitive exams and mental math, use this order:

---

# STEP 1 — Eliminate Easy Numbers

## Even?

If ends in:

0, 2, 4, 6, 8

→ NOT PRIME  
(except 2)

---

## Divisible by 3?

Add digits.

Example:

147

1+4+7 = 12

12 divisible by 3  
→ 147 NOT PRIME

---

## Ends in 5 or 0?

→ NOT PRIME  
(except 5)

---

# STEP 2 — Find Approximate Square Root

You only check primes below square root.

Example:

137

[  
\sqrt{137} \approx 11.7  
]

So only test:

2, 3, 5, 7, 11

---

# STEP 3 — Use Only Prime Divisors

Do NOT test all numbers.

Only test:

2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17...

---

# REAL SHORTCUT

After removing:

- even numbers
    
- multiples of 3
    
- multiples of 5
    

Most remaining composite numbers are divisible by:

7, 11, 13

So usually check in this order:

2 → 3 → 5 → 7 → 11 → 13

That solves most questions fast.

---

# Example 1 — Is 97 Prime?

- odd ✅
    
- 9+7=16 → not divisible by 3 ✅
    
- not ending in 5 ✅
    

[  
\sqrt{97} \approx 9.8  
]

Check only:

2,3,5,7

97 not divisible by any.

→ PRIME

---

# Example 2 — Is 221 Prime?

- odd ✅
    
- 2+2+1=5 → not divisible by 3 ✅
    
- not ending in 5 ✅
    

[  
\sqrt{221} \approx 14.8  
]

Check:

7 → no

11 → no

13:

221 ÷ 13 = 17

→ NOT PRIME

---

# SUPER FAST TRICK

All primes greater than 3 are of form:

6n\pm1

Examples:

5 = 6(1)-1  
7 = 6(1)+1  
11 = 6(2)-1  
13 = 6(2)+1

If number is NOT in this form → definitely not prime.

BUT:

Being 6n±1 does NOT guarantee prime.

Example:

25 = 6(4)+1  
But 25 not prime.

So this is only a filter trick.

---

# Ultimate Exam Shortcut

For numbers below 500:

1. Eliminate:
    
    - even
        
    - divisible by 3
        
    - ending in 5
        
2. Check primes only up to square root.
    

That's the fastest reliable method manually.
