A **fraction** has two parts:

[  
\frac{\text{Numerator}}{\text{Denominator}}  
]

- **Numerator** = top number
    
- **Denominator** = bottom number
    

Example:

[  
\frac{3}{5}  
]

Here, 3 is the numerator and 5 is the denominator.

---

## Proper Fractions

A **proper fraction** is a fraction where the numerator is **smaller** than the denominator.

Examples:

[  
\frac{1}{2}, \frac{3}{4}, \frac{5}{8}  
]

These fractions are always **less than 1**.

Example using a math block:

\frac{3}{4}

---

## Improper Fractions

An **improper fraction** is a fraction where the numerator is **greater than or equal to** the denominator.

Examples:

[  
\frac{5}{4}, \frac{7}{3}, \frac{9}{9}  
]

These fractions are **greater than or equal to 1**.

Example:

\frac{7}{3}

---

## Quick Comparison

|Proper Fraction|Improper Fraction|
|---|---|
|Numerator < Denominator|Numerator ≥ Denominator|
|Value is less than 1|Value is 1 or more|
|Example: ( \frac{2}{5} )|Example: ( \frac{8}{5} )|
