# Dividing Fractions

Rule:

# $$  
\frac{a}{b} \div \frac{c}{d}

\frac{a}{b} \times \frac{d}{c}  
$$

**Keep, Change, Flip**

1. Keep the first fraction.
    
2. Change ÷ to ×.
    
3. Flip the second fraction.
    

---

## Example 1

$$  
\frac{1}{2} \div \frac{3}{4}  
$$

Keep, Change, Flip:

$$  
\frac{1}{2} \times \frac{4}{3}  
$$

Multiply:

# $$  
\frac{1 \times 4}{2 \times 3}

# \frac{4}{6}

\frac{2}{3}  
$$

Answer:

$$  
\frac{2}{3}  
$$

---

## Example 2

$$  
\frac{3}{5} \div \frac{2}{7}  
$$

Keep, Change, Flip:

$$  
\frac{3}{5} \times \frac{7}{2}  
$$

Multiply:

$$  
\frac{21}{10}  
$$

Answer:

$$  
\frac{21}{10}  
$$

---

## Example 3

$$  
\frac{4}{9} \div \frac{2}{3}  
$$

Keep, Change, Flip:

$$  
\frac{4}{9} \times \frac{3}{2}  
$$

Cross-cancel:

$$  
4 \div 2 = 2  
$$

$$  
3 \div 3 = 1  
$$

Multiply:

$$  
\frac{2}{3}  
$$

Answer:

$$  
\frac{2}{3}  
$$

# Fraction to Decimal

Rule:

$$  
\text{Decimal} = \text{Numerator} \div \text{Denominator}  
$$

Examples:

$$  
\frac{1}{2} = 1 \div 2 = 0.5  
$$

$$  
\frac{3}{4} = 3 \div 4 = 0.75  
$$

$$  
\frac{1}{5} = 1 \div 5 = 0.2  
$$

---

# Fraction to Percentage

Rule:

$$  
\text{Percentage} = \text{Fraction} \times 100  
$$

Example:

$$  
\frac{3}{4} \times 100 = 75%  
$$

---
