# Addition of Fractions

## Same Denominator

Keep the denominator the same and add the numerators.

Example:

\frac{2}{7}+\frac{3}{7}=\frac{5}{7}

Steps:

1. Add 2 + 3 = 5
    
2. Keep denominator 7
    

Answer = **5/7**

---

## Different Denominators

Find the **LCM** of the denominators.

Example:

\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{3}=\frac{3}{6}+\frac{2}{6}=\frac{5}{6}

Steps:

1. LCM of 2 and 3 = 6
    
2. Convert fractions to denominator 6
    
3. Add numerators
    

Answer = **5/6**

---

# Subtraction of Fractions

## Same Denominator

Keep the denominator the same and subtract the numerators.

Example:

\frac{5}{8}-\frac{2}{8}=\frac{3}{8}

Steps:

1. Subtract 5 − 2 = 3
    
2. Keep denominator 8
    

Answer = **3/8**

---

## Different Denominators

Find the LCM first.

Example:

\frac{3}{4}-\frac{1}{6}=\frac{9}{12}-\frac{2}{12}=\frac{7}{12}

Steps:

1. LCM of 4 and 6 = 12
    
2. Convert both fractions to denominator 12
    
3. Subtract numerators
    

Answer = **7/12**

The rule is simple:

# [  
\frac{a}{b}\times\frac{c}{d}

\frac{a\times c}{b\times d}  
]

Example:

\frac{2}{3}\times\frac{4}{5}=\frac{8}{15}

Multiply the numerators:

- (2 \times 4 = 8)
    

Multiply the denominators:

- (3 \times 5 = 15)
    

Answer = **8/15**

---

## Multiplying a Fraction by a Whole Number

Treat the whole number as a fraction with denominator 1.

Example:

# [  
\frac{3}{4}\times 2

# \frac{3}{4}\times\frac{2}{1}

# \frac{6}{4}

\frac{3}{2}  
]

---

## Cross-Cancellation (Shortcut)

Before multiplying, cancel common factors.

Example:

[  
\frac{2}{3}\times\frac{9}{10}  
]

Cancel:

- 2 and 10 → 1 and 5
    
- 9 and 3 → 3 and 1
    

Then:

# [  
\frac{1\times3}{1\times5}

\frac{3}{5}  
]

Much easier than multiplying first.
