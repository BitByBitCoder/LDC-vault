## Divisibility Rule for 11

**Alternately add and subtract digits from left to right. If the result is 0 or divisible by 11, the number is divisible by 11.**

---

### Example: Is 2728 divisible by 11?

Digits: 2, 7, 2, 8

2 − 7 + 2 − 8 = **−11** ✅ → Yes. 

---

### Example: Is 1364 divisible by 11?

1 − 3 + 6 − 4 = **0** ✅ → Yes.

---

### Example: Is 521 divisible by 11?

5 − 2 + 1 = **4** ❌ → No.

---

### Another way to think about it:

> **Sum of digits in ODD positions − Sum of digits in EVEN positions = 0 or multiple of 11**

For **2728**: Odd positions (1st, 3rd) = 2 + 2 = 4 | Even positions (2nd, 4th) = 7 + 8 = 15 4 − 15 = −11 ✅

---

**Easy to remember:** 11 × 11 = 121 → 1 − 2 + 1 = **0** ✓
